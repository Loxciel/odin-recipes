Rosemary Focaccia

Description

A delicious focaccia recipe that can be prepared and made in one day!
This recipe uses just rosemary but you can add any herb or vegetable that you like!

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil to mix in the starter
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 3/4 cup of bread flour (all-purpose OK)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Extra olive oil for dressing the dough
  • Fresh or dried rosemary to taste.
  • (Optional) Vegetables to top before placing in oven.

Steps

  1. Make sure the yeast has bloomed first- water that is too hot will ruin the yeast.
    Mix the water, yeast, 1tsp olive oil, and sugar.
  2. Mix in flour , rosemary, and salt until fully incorporated- no dry patches should remain.
  3. Add a splash of olive oil on top and cover with plastic wrap.
  4. Set in fridge and let rise for 10-16 hours OR 5-6 hours in a warm place.
  5. Transfer and spread proofed bread into a baking dish with 5-6 teaspoons of olive oil.
  6. Cover and let proof for 4-5 hours in a warm place.
  7. Top with extra herbs, vegetables, toppings if desired.
  8. Drizzle olive oil over the proofed dough and create dimples by pressing down with fingers.
  9. Preheat oven to 420F and bake for 35-40 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
